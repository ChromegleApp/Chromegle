Why should I care?

Omegle abuses a vulnerability in browsers known as the WebRTC Leak to detect your real IP address when you are on a VPN and are using your microphone/webcam.

This issue only happens with poor quality VPN providers that "pretend" to be a VPN while only sending your HTTP traffic through proxies (browser extension VPNs).

The VPN you are using currently is vulnerable to the WebRTC leak, and Omegle can tell you are on a VPN. Because of this, they more than likely will block you from being able to connect to video chats on Omegle.

What should I do?

Change VPN providers. In the interest of remaining a neutral party, Chromegle will not suggest a specific provider to use.

We have instead compiled a definitive list of providers both vulnerable and not vulnerable to this issue. This uses third-party data and may be outdated, so please only use it as an advisory when testing your own VPNs against this issue.

When you switch VPNs, to check if you have fixed the issue reload Omegle and start a video chat. If this menu doesn't show up anymore, you're good to go!

Finally, DO NOT disable WebRTC. The goal is for your VPN to "leak" your fake IP address. If it doesn't leak any address (i.e if it detects you trying to protect yourself), Omegle will block you anyways.
